King Bhumibol the Great - Rama IX

Key events in the life of Bhumibol Adulyadej

  • 1927 - Born on 5 December 1927 at Cambridge Hospital in Cambridge, Massachusetts USA, the only monarch born in the US as his father, Prince Mahidol Adulyadej, was enrolled in the public health program at Harvard University.
  • 1946 - He ascended the throne after his brother, King Ananda Mahidol, was found dead in his palace bedchamber from a bullet wound to the head on 9 June 1946.
  • 1950 - Bhumibol was crowned King of Thailand on May 5 1950 after long delays in the Grand Palace in Bangkok.
  • 1957 - A military coup overthrew the government of Field Marshal Plaek Phibunsongkhram. This began a new period of strong relations between the monarch and the military.
  • 1975 - Following the rise of Thailand's military dictatorship, guerilla and communist movements began threatening the monarchy and political establishment. Bhumibol used this as a pretext to back a military coup.
  • 1992 - Bhumibol intervened between a military dictatorship and pro-democracy movement, summoning the leaders of each faction which ultimately lead to a civilian government.
  • 2006 - 2015 - Throughout this period he suffered from declining health ranging from lumbar spinal stenosis, Parkinson's disease, and depression.
  • 2016 - King Bhumibol Adulyadej died in Bangkok on October 13 2016. Following his burial was a mourning period of Thai citizens for 1 year.
